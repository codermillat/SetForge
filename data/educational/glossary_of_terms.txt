# Context: Glossary of Indian Academic and Administrative Terms

- **AKTU (Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University):** The state government's technical university in Uttar Pradesh. Many private engineering colleges, like G.L. Bajaj, are affiliated with it and it awards their degrees.
- **NAAC (National Assessment and Accreditation Council):** An autonomous body funded by the UGC that assesses and accredits higher education institutions in India. 'A+' is a very high grade, indicating excellent quality.
- **NIRF (National Institutional Ranking Framework):** A methodology adopted by the Indian government's Ministry of Education to rank institutions of higher education in India.
- **UGC (University Grants Commission):** The primary regulatory body for higher education in India. A "UGC-recognized" degree is valid and accepted.
- **FRRO (Foreigners Regional Registration Office):** The government agency responsible for registering and tracking international visitors, including students. Registration is a mandatory legal requirement.
- **Lateral Entry:** A process that allows students with a 3-year diploma to get direct admission into the second year of a 4-year B.Tech program.
- **SAARC (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation):** An economic and political organization of eight countries in South Asia. Universities often have special fee structures or policies for students from SAARC nations (including Bangladesh).
- **Bonafide Certificate:** An official certificate issued by the university to certify that a student is enrolled in a specific course. It is used for various official purposes, including opening bank accounts and sometimes for internships.
